# Workplace Code of Conduct

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Applies To:** All employees, contractors, interns, and volunteers of Acme Corp
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources Department

## 1. Purpose

Acme Corp is committed to fostering a respectful, inclusive, and professional workplace where all individuals can do their best work. This Code of Conduct establishes the standards of behavior expected of everyone in our organization.

All employees are required to read, understand, and comply with this policy. Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

## 2. Professional Behavior

### 2.1 General Expectations
All Acme Corp team members are expected to:
- Treat colleagues, clients, and partners with dignity and respect
- Communicate honestly, constructively, and professionally
- Be punctual and prepared for meetings and commitments
- Take ownership of their work and follow through on commitments
- Support and mentor colleagues, especially new team members
- Maintain a clean and safe workspace (in-office and shared spaces)

### 2.2 Collaboration & Communication
- Assume positive intent in communications, especially in written/async formats
- Provide constructive feedback focused on work, not personal attributes
- Disagree respectfully -- vigorous debate about ideas is encouraged; personal attacks are not
- Be mindful of cultural differences in communication styles
- Include relevant stakeholders in decisions that affect their work

### 2.3 Professionalism in Remote Settings
- Dress appropriately for video calls (business casual minimum for external meetings)
- Ensure your background is professional or use a virtual background
- Be fully present during meetings -- avoid multitasking

## 3. Anti-Harassment Policy

### 3.1 Zero Tolerance
Acme Corp has a **zero-tolerance policy** for harassment of any kind. Harassment includes, but is not limited to:

- **Sexual harassment:** Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, sexually suggestive comments, inappropriate touching, sharing of sexual content
- **Verbal harassment:** Slurs, epithets, derogatory comments, intimidation, threats, bullying
- **Visual harassment:** Displaying offensive images, cartoons, or gestures
- **Cyber harassment:** Sending harassing messages via email, Slack, social media, or other digital channels
- **Retaliation:** Any adverse action against someone who reports harassment or participates in an investigation

### 3.2 Protected Characteristics
Harassment based on any of the following is strictly prohibited:
- Race, color, ethnicity, or national origin
- Gender, gender identity, or gender expression
- Sexual orientation
- Age
- Disability (physical or mental)
- Religion or beliefs
- Pregnancy or parental status
- Veteran status
- Marital status
- Genetic information
- Any other characteristic protected by applicable law

### 3.3 Scope
This policy applies to all work-related settings, including:
- Office premises and company events
- Remote work environments (video calls, chat, email)
- Business travel and off-site meetings
- Company-sponsored social events
- Online interactions on company platforms

## 4. Reporting Procedures

### 4.1 How to Report
If you experience or witness harassment, discrimination, or any violation of this Code of Conduct, you have multiple reporting options:

1. **Direct manager:** Speak to your manager if you feel comfortable doing so.
2. **HR Department:** Contact Emily Davis (emily.davis@acmecorp.com) or any HR team member.
3. **Anonymous Hotline:** Call **1-800-555-ETHIC** (available 24/7, calls are confidential and may be anonymous).
4. **Online reporting:** Submit a report at https://ethics.acmecorp.com (anonymous option available).
5. **Skip-level reporting:** If your concern involves your manager, contact their manager or HR directly.

### 4.2 Investigation Process
All reports are taken seriously and investigated promptly:
1. **Acknowledgment:** HR will acknowledge receipt of the report within 1 business day.
2. **Investigation:** A trained HR investigator will conduct interviews and gather evidence. Typical investigations are completed within 10-15 business days.
3. **Confidentiality:** Information is shared only on a need-to-know basis. Both the reporter and the accused are entitled to privacy protections.
4. **Resolution:** Findings and any corrective actions will be communicated to relevant parties.
5. **Appeal:** Either party may appeal the findings to the VP of HR within 10 business days.

### 4.3 No Retaliation
Acme Corp strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who:
- Makes a good-faith report of misconduct
- Participates in an investigation
- Refuses to participate in conduct they believe is illegal or unethical

Retaliation is a separate violation and will result in disciplinary action.

## 5. Social Media Policy

### 5.1 Personal Social Media
Employees are free to use personal social media, with these guidelines:
- Do not disclose confidential company information (financial results, product plans, customer data)
- Make clear that personal opinions are your own, not Acme Corp's position
- Do not use the Acme Corp logo or brand in personal posts without approval
- Do not engage in online harassment of colleagues, competitors, or customers
- Be mindful that your online presence can reflect on the company

### 5.2 Official Company Accounts
Only authorized employees (Marketing team, designated spokespersons) may post on behalf of Acme Corp. All official posts must be approved by the Marketing Director.

### 5.3 Media Inquiries
If contacted by media for comment about Acme Corp, do not respond directly. Forward all media inquiries to the Communications team at press@acmecorp.com.

## 6. Confidentiality & Intellectual Property

### 6.1 Confidential Information
Employees must protect all confidential information, including:
- Business strategies, financial data, and projections
- Customer and partner data, contracts, and pricing
- Product roadmaps, technical designs, and source code
- Employee personal information (salaries, reviews, medical data)
- Board meeting minutes and investor communications

### 6.2 Handling Confidential Information
- Store confidential documents in approved company systems (Google Drive, Confluence) -- not personal devices or cloud storage
- Use encryption when sharing sensitive data electronically
- Shred physical documents containing confidential information
- Lock your screen when away from your workstation
- Do not discuss confidential matters in public spaces

### 6.3 Intellectual Property
- All work product created during employment belongs to Acme Corp (per your employment agreement)
- Obtain written permission before using company IP for personal projects, publications, or speaking engagements
- Report any potential IP conflicts to your manager and Legal immediately

### 6.4 After Employment
Confidentiality obligations continue after leaving Acme Corp:
- Return all company materials, documents, and equipment
- Do not share confidential information with new employers
- Non-compete and non-solicitation terms (if applicable) remain in effect per your employment agreement

## 7. Conflicts of Interest

### 7.1 Definition
A conflict of interest occurs when personal interests could interfere with your ability to act in Acme Corp's best interest. Examples include:
- Having a financial interest in a competitor, supplier, or customer
- Hiring or supervising a family member or close personal friend
- Accepting gifts or entertainment that could influence business decisions
- Engaging in outside employment that competes with or interferes with your Acme Corp duties

### 7.2 Disclosure
If you have a potential conflict of interest, disclose it to your manager and HR **before** any related business activity. Disclosure does not necessarily mean the activity is prohibited -- it allows us to manage the situation transparently.

### 7.3 Gifts & Entertainment
- **Receiving:** Gifts valued at $50 or less may be accepted. Gifts over $50 must be reported to HR and may need to be declined or donated to the company.
- **Giving:** Business gifts to clients or partners must be approved by your manager and comply with anti-bribery laws.

## 8. Substance Abuse

- The use of illegal drugs on company premises or during work hours is prohibited.
- Alcohol consumption is permitted at company-sponsored events in moderation.
- Employees must not be under the influence of drugs or alcohol while performing work duties.
- Employees who need help with substance abuse issues are encouraged to contact the Employee Assistance Program (EAP) confidentially.

## 9. Disciplinary Process

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:
1. **Verbal warning** -- documented in employee file
2. **Written warning** -- formal notice with specific improvement expectations and timeline
3. **Suspension** -- with or without pay, pending investigation or as a corrective measure
4. **Termination** -- for serious violations or repeated offenses

Severe violations (violence, theft, fraud, sexual assault) may result in immediate termination without prior warnings.
