# Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Applies To:** All full-time employees of Acme Corp
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources Department

## 1. Overview

Acme Corp believes that time away from work is essential for employee well-being, creativity, and sustained performance. This policy outlines the types of paid time off available, accrual rates, and the procedures for requesting leave.

## 2. PTO Accrual Rates

PTO accrual is based on years of continuous service at Acme Corp:

| Tenure | Annual Vacation Days | Accrual Rate (per month) |
|--------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| 0-2 years | 15 days | 1.25 days/month |
| 2-5 years | 20 days | 1.67 days/month |
| 5+ years | 25 days | 2.08 days/month |

**Notes:**
- New hires begin accruing PTO from their first day of employment.
- First-year allocation is prorated based on start date.
- Tenure is calculated from the employee's original hire date, including any rehire scenarios within 12 months.

## 3. Types of Leave

### 3.1 Vacation Days
- Used for personal time off, travel, family events, or any reason.
- Must be requested in advance (see Section 5).
- Can be taken in full-day or half-day increments.

### 3.2 Sick Days
- All employees receive **10 sick days** per calendar year.
- Used for personal illness, medical appointments, or caring for an immediate family member.
- Sick days do not carry over to the next year; unused days expire on December 31.
- A doctor's note is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days.

### 3.3 Personal Days
- All employees receive **5 personal days** per calendar year.
- Used for personal matters such as moving, legal appointments, or family obligations.
- Personal days do not carry over to the next year.

## 4. Carryover Rules

- **Vacation days:** A maximum of **5 unused vacation days** may be carried over to the next calendar year. Carried-over days must be used by March 31 or they will expire.
- **Sick days:** No carryover. Balance resets to 10 on January 1.
- **Personal days:** No carryover. Balance resets to 5 on January 1.

## 5. Requesting Time Off

### 5.1 Approval Process
1. Submit a leave request through the HR system or HR assistant.
2. Your direct manager will review and approve/deny the request.
3. Requests for 5+ consecutive days require both manager and department head approval.
4. You will receive a confirmation once approved.

### 5.2 Notice Requirements
- **Vacation (1-2 days):** At least 1 week advance notice.
- **Vacation (3+ days):** At least 2 weeks advance notice.
- **Vacation (1+ week):** At least 4 weeks advance notice.
- **Sick leave:** Notify your manager as soon as possible, ideally before your scheduled start time.
- **Personal days:** At least 3 business days advance notice when possible.

### 5.3 Manager Responsibilities
- Respond to leave requests within 2 business days.
- Consider team coverage and project deadlines when approving.
- Cannot unreasonably deny requests that follow the notice requirements.

## 6. Blackout Periods

The following periods are designated as **blackout periods** during which vacation requests are generally not approved unless pre-arranged:

- **December 20 - January 2** (holiday season / year-end close)
- **Last 5 business days of fiscal year** (June 25-30)

Exceptions may be granted on a case-by-case basis by department heads. Sick leave is always available regardless of blackout periods.

## 7. Company Holidays

Acme Corp observes the following **10 paid holidays** in addition to PTO:

1. New Year's Day (January 1)
2. Martin Luther King Jr. Day (3rd Monday in January)
3. Presidents' Day (3rd Monday in February)
4. Memorial Day (Last Monday in May)
5. Independence Day (July 4)
6. Labor Day (1st Monday in September)
7. Thanksgiving Day (4th Thursday in November)
8. Day After Thanksgiving (4th Friday in November)
9. Christmas Eve (December 24)
10. Christmas Day (December 25)

When a holiday falls on a weekend, the preceding Friday (Saturday holiday) or following Monday (Sunday holiday) will be observed.

## 8. Unpaid Leave

If an employee exhausts all available PTO, additional time off may be taken as unpaid leave with manager and HR approval. Extended unpaid leave (more than 5 days) requires VP-level approval and may affect benefits eligibility.

## 9. PTO Upon Termination

- Unused vacation days will be paid out upon voluntary or involuntary termination.
- Sick and personal days are not paid out upon termination.
- Employees who resign must provide standard notice (2 weeks) and cannot use PTO during the notice period without manager approval.
